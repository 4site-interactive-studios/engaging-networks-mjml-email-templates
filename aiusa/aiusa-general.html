FEELING INSPIRED? ACT NOW

We are shocked and alarmed by all incidents of gun violence we’ve been seeing. Our government has failed to confront the crisis of gun violence, including the racialized and gendered violence that upholds white supremacy — and has made access to guns far too easy. The Amnesty movement grieves with these communities, and we are clearer than ever on this basic truth: living free from violence is a human right.

Amnesty is actively calling for a prompt, thorough, transparent, and impartial examination of all the circumstances of the spa shootings in the Atlanta area, including whether the shootings qualify as "Hate Crimes" under Georgia law, which imposes additional penalties for crimes motivated by a victim's race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender or disability.

And we're calling on our allies and supporters to join us in urging Congress to pass the Break the Cycle of Violence Act, which aims to reduce gun violence nationwide by supporting community-based violence reduction programs. This would be a MAJOR step toward our building a country free of gun violence.

Help hold your representatives accountable: Tell them to support the Break the Cycle of Violence Act today »
